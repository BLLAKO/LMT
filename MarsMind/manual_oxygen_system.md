# Oxygen Generation System Manual (excerpt)

## Section 5.1 - Normal operation
The system electrolyzes water to produce oxygen. Normal output pressure
is 14.5-15.5 psi. A soft cycling sound every 90 seconds is normal.

## Section 5.2 - Output pressure drop
Symptoms: gauge shows below 14 psi, no unusual smell, cycling sound
sounds normal.
Likely cause: partial blockage in the output line, or a small drop in
feed water supply.
Safety warning: If pressure drops below 12 psi, switch to backup oxygen
supply immediately before troubleshooting.
Repair steps:
1. Check feed water tank level first; refill if below 20 percent.
2. If feed water is adequate, inspect the output line for kinks or ice
   buildup (common in cold-side components).
3. Clear any blockage found.
4. Restart the electrolysis cycle and monitor pressure for 5 minutes.

## Section 5.3 - Unusual odor during cycling
Symptoms: a sharp or chemical smell during the cycling sound, pressure
reading may be normal or slightly elevated.
Likely cause: possible seal degradation allowing hydrogen byproduct
cross-contamination.
Safety warning: This is a high-priority fault. Do not continue normal
operation. Switch to backup oxygen supply and power down the unit before
any inspection. Ventilate the area if possible.
Repair steps:
1. Switch to backup oxygen supply.
2. Power down the electrolysis unit at the breaker.
3. Do not reopen the unit without confirming hydrogen sensor reads zero.
4. This fault requires flagging to Earth before any repair is attempted,
   due to contamination risk.

## Section 5.4 - Intermittent output fluctuation
Symptoms: pressure gauge fluctuates between 13 and 16 psi over several
minutes, no smell, no unusual sound.
Likely cause: normal variation tied to feed water temperature; usually
not a fault.
Safety warning: none.
Repair steps:
1. Monitor for 15 minutes.
2. If fluctuation stays within 13-16 psi range, no action needed.
3. If it drops below 12 psi at any point, treat as Section 5.2.
