# Water Recycler Manual (excerpt)

## Section 3.1 - Normal operation
The recycler filters and purifies water continuously. Normal flow rate is
4-6 liters per hour. A faint hum is normal during filtration cycles.

## Section 3.2 - Clogged pressure filter
Symptoms: weak or reduced water flow, a rattling or knocking noise from
the recycler housing, pressure gauge reading above 80 psi.
Likely cause: debris buildup in the pressure filter mesh.
Safety warning: Do not open the filter housing while pressure is above 40 psi.
Doing so can cause rapid depressurization and injury.
Repair steps:
1. Enter maintenance mode from the control panel.
2. Close Valve A to isolate the filter section.
3. Confirm pressure gauge reads below 40 psi before proceeding.
4. Turn Valve B counterclockwise to release residual pressure.
5. Remove and clean the filter mesh.
6. Reinstall the filter mesh and reverse steps 2-4.
7. Restart the system and confirm flow rate returns to 4-6 L/hr.

## Section 3.3 - Pump seal leak
Symptoms: slow, steady drip near the base of the unit, gradual water level
drop in the storage tank, no unusual noise.
Likely cause: worn pump seal.
Safety warning: none beyond standard electrical caution near the pump motor.
Repair steps:
1. Power down the recycler fully at the breaker.
2. Locate the pump seal access panel on the lower left of the unit.
3. Replace the seal using the spare parts kit, item WR-004.
4. Power on and monitor for 10 minutes for continued leaking.

## Section 3.4 - Sensor fault (false low-flow warning)
Symptoms: system reports low flow, but manual inspection shows normal
flow and no unusual noise or pressure reading.
Likely cause: flow sensor miscalibration.
Safety warning: none.
Repair steps:
1. Access the sensor calibration menu from the control panel.
2. Run the auto-calibration routine.
3. If the warning persists after calibration, replace the sensor unit,
   item WR-011.
